SOFTWARE SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Software Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 1, 2026,
between Acme Technologies Ltd, a company incorporated in England and Wales
("Service Provider"), and GlobalCorp Inc, a corporation organised under the laws of
Delaware, USA ("Client").

1. SERVICES
   Service Provider agrees to provide cloud-based data analytics software and related
   professional services as described in Schedule A attached hereto ("Services").
   Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner
   consistent with industry standards.

2. DATA PROCESSING AND PRIVACY
   2.1 The parties acknowledge that Service Provider may process personal data on behalf
   of Client in connection with the Services. Such processing shall be governed by the
   Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") attached as Exhibit B, which incorporates the
   Standard Contractual Clauses (EU Commission Implementing Decision 2021/914) and the
   UK International Data Transfer Addendum (IDTA) for cross-border transfers.

   2.2 Service Provider shall:
   (a) Process personal data solely on documented instructions from Client;
   (b) Retain personal data only for the duration necessary to deliver the Services,
       and in no event longer than 24 months after termination of this Agreement,
       after which all personal data shall be deleted or returned to Client;
   (c) Implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures including
       AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit for all personal data;
   (d) Notify Client within 72 hours of becoming aware of any personal data breach,
       as required by GDPR Article 33 and applicable US state laws;
   (e) Not use Client personal data for Service Provider's own product improvement
       or any purpose beyond delivering the contracted Services.

   2.3 Client shall have all rights afforded to data subjects under GDPR, HIPAA,
   CCPA, and applicable data protection laws, including the right to access, correct,
   delete, and port their personal data upon written request.

3. INDEMNIFICATION
   3.1 Each party ("Indemnifying Party") shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless
   the other party ("Indemnified Party") from and against third-party claims arising
   from the Indemnifying Party's: (a) breach of this Agreement; (b) gross negligence
   or wilful misconduct; or (c) infringement of a third party's intellectual property
   rights in connection with such party's own technology or content.

   3.2 The aggregate indemnification obligation of either party shall not exceed the
   total fees paid or payable by Client to Service Provider in the twelve (12) months
   immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

   3.3 Indemnification obligations are mutual and apply equally to both parties.

4. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
   4.1 Each party's total aggregate liability arising out of or related to this
   Agreement shall not exceed the greater of: (a) the total fees paid by Client in the
   twelve (12) months prior to the event giving rise to the claim; or (b) USD 50,000.

   4.2 Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential,
   or punitive damages, provided that this limitation is mutual and applies equally to
   both parties.

   4.3 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the limitations in Sections 4.1 and 4.2 shall
   NOT apply to: (a) a party's indemnification obligations under Section 3;
   (b) breach of confidentiality obligations; (c) infringement of intellectual property
   rights; (d) gross negligence or wilful misconduct; or (e) death or personal injury
   caused by negligence.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
   5.1 Pre-Existing IP. Each party retains all ownership of its pre-existing
   intellectual property ("Background IP"). Nothing in this Agreement transfers any
   Background IP from one party to the other.

   5.2 Work Product. All work product, deliverables, and materials specifically created
   by Service Provider for Client under this Agreement ("Work Product") shall be owned
   by Client upon full payment of applicable fees. Service Provider hereby assigns all
   right, title, and interest in such Work Product to Client.

   5.3 Service Provider License. Service Provider grants Client a non-exclusive,
   non-transferable, limited licence to use Service Provider's Background IP solely
   to the extent necessary to receive and use the Services during the Term.

   5.4 IP Indemnification. Service Provider shall indemnify Client against third-party
   claims alleging that the Services infringe any patent, copyright, or trade secret,
   subject to Client promptly notifying Service Provider of such claims.

   5.5 Each party represents that it has no prior conflicting obligations to third
   parties that would prevent it from fulfilling its IP obligations under this Agreement,
   consistent with the principles established in Stanford v. Roche (US Supreme Court 2011).

6. CONFIDENTIALITY
   6.1 Each party ("Receiving Party") agrees to keep confidential all non-public
   information disclosed by the other party ("Disclosing Party") that is designated
   as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential
   ("Confidential Information").

   6.2 This confidentiality obligation shall remain in effect for three (3) years
   following termination of this Agreement. Trade secrets shall be protected for as
   long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.

   6.3 Standard Exceptions. The obligations in Section 6.1 do not apply to information
   that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
   (b) was known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently
   developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information; or
   (d) is required to be disclosed by applicable law, court order, or regulatory
   authority, provided the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice to the
   Disclosing Party where legally permitted.

   6.4 Nothing in this Agreement restricts either party's employees from exercising
   rights protected under applicable labour laws, including the right to discuss
   working conditions, report violations to regulatory authorities, or engage in
   protected concerted activity as recognised by the NLRB and Section 7 of the NLRA.

   6.5 Upon termination, each party shall promptly return or destroy the other party's
   Confidential Information and certify such destruction in writing within 30 days.

7. TERMINATION
   7.1 Term. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an
   initial term of one (1) year, automatically renewing for successive one-year terms
   unless either party provides at least 60 days written notice of non-renewal.

   7.2 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement for
   any reason upon providing 30 days written notice to the other party.

   7.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately
   upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and
   fails to cure such breach within 30 days of written notice; (b) becomes insolvent
   or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (c) ceases to conduct
   business in the ordinary course.

   7.4 Effect of Termination. Upon termination, Service Provider shall deliver to
   Client all Work Product, data, and Confidential Information within 30 days.
   Clauses 5 (IP), 6 (Confidentiality), 4 (Liability), 3 (Indemnification), and
   11 (Dispute Resolution) shall survive termination.

8. NON-COMPETE
   8.1 During the Term of this Agreement, neither party shall solicit or hire the
   other party's key personnel directly involved in performing the Services without
   prior written consent.

   8.2 This Agreement does not contain post-termination non-compete restrictions on
   Client's general business activities. The parties acknowledge that overbroad
   non-compete obligations may be unenforceable under applicable law, including
   California Business & Professions Code Section 16600 and the FTC's 2024 guidance
   on non-compete clauses.

9. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION
   9.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws
   of the State of New York, USA, without regard to its conflict of law provisions.

   9.2 The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods
   (CISG) is expressly excluded from this Agreement.

   9.3 Each party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of
   New York, New York for the resolution of disputes not subject to arbitration.

10. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
    10.1 Escalation. The parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute
    through direct negotiation between senior representatives within 30 days of written
    notice of a dispute.

    10.2 Mediation. If direct negotiation fails, the parties shall attempt mediation
    before a mutually agreed mediator within 30 days.

    10.3 Arbitration. If mediation fails, disputes shall be finally resolved by
    binding arbitration administered by JAMS under its Comprehensive Arbitration Rules.
    The seat of arbitration shall be New York, New York. The language of arbitration
    shall be English.

    10.4 Emergency Relief. Notwithstanding Section 10.3, either party retains the
    right to seek emergency injunctive or other equitable relief from a court of
    competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm pending arbitration.

    10.5 Class Actions. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to waive any
    party's rights under applicable consumer protection or labour laws, including
    class action rights where such waivers are not permitted by law.

11. FINANCIAL RECORDS AND AUDIT
    11.1 Both parties shall retain all financial records, audit logs, and documentation
    related to this Agreement for a minimum of seven (7) years after the close of each
    fiscal year, in compliance with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) Section 802.

    11.2 In the event either party receives notice of a legal hold, investigation, or
    regulatory inquiry, all document destruction obligations are immediately suspended
    and a litigation hold shall be applied to all potentially relevant records.

    11.3 Service Provider shall provide Client with reasonable access to relevant
    financial records and audit logs upon 10 business days written notice, no more than
    once per calendar year.

12. WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTIONS
    12.1 Nothing in this Agreement shall prohibit, restrict, or penalise any person
    from: (a) reporting potential violations of law to any governmental agency or
    regulatory authority, including the SEC, OSHA, or the NLRB; (b) making disclosures
    protected under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act Section 806, the Dodd-Frank Act, or any
    applicable whistleblower protection law; or (c) cooperating with a government
    investigation.

    12.2 The parties shall not retaliate against any individual who makes a protected
    disclosure under applicable whistleblower statutes.

14. PAYMENT TERMS
    14.1 Client shall pay all undisputed invoices within Net 30 days of receipt.
    14.2 Late payments shall accrue interest at the lesser of 1.5% per month or the
    maximum rate permitted by applicable law.
    14.3 Client may dispute invoices in good faith by written notice within 15 days
    of receipt, and the parties shall resolve disputes within 30 days.

    15.2 Amendment. This Agreement may only be amended by a written instrument signed
    by authorised representatives of both parties.

    15.3 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or
    unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

    15.4 Force Majeure. Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures in
    performance resulting from causes beyond its reasonable control, including acts of

    15.5 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which
